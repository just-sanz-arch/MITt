Creative Art's and Design

In 1972, Jerome B. Wiesner, the 13th president of MIT, established the Council to strengthen the arts at MIT and to expand arts funding. As MIT president, Wiesner transformed the Institute, recognizing that being the best in science and engineering was not enough–that tomorrow’s leaders also need to integrate the arts and humanities into their thinking about engineering, science, technology and policy. For the celebration of the 40th anniversary of CAMIT’s founding, the Council commissioned a short film about its history.

Architecture and Development

MIT's Department of Architecture, established in 1865, is one of the oldest architecture programs in the United States. It was the first to offer a formal architecture curriculum within a university setting. The program has a rich history of innovation and experimentation, with a focus on the intersection of architecture, technology, and social issues. Notable alumni include I. M. Pei, Kevin Roche, and Steven Holl. In recent years, the department has continued to push the boundaries of architectural education, incorporating digital tools and sustainable design principles into its curriculum. It offers a rigorous and interdisciplinary approach, challenging students to be creative, innovative, and responsible leaders in the field.

Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS)

Architecture: * Established: 1865, one of the oldest architecture programs in the US. * Focus: The intersection of architecture, technology, and social issues. * Notable alumni: I.M. Pei, Kevin Roche, Steven Holl. * Innovation: Continuously adopting the latest technology and sustainable design principles. Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS): * Established: 1882, the first electrical engineering program in the US. * Development: From focusing on basic electricity to becoming a center of innovation in computers and digital technology. * Contribution: The development of the internet, personal computers, and other technologies. * Present: One of the world's leading EECS departments, focused on research and innovation. In essence: Both departments at MIT have a long and rich history of innovation, and have made significant contributions to their respective fields.

Medical

MIT doesn't offer a traditional undergraduate medical degree like an MD. However, it's renowned for its contributions to biomedical research and engineering. MIT's involvement in medical education and research primarily occurs through the Harvard-MIT Program in Health Sciences and Technology (HST). This joint program offers: * MD Program: Trains physician-scientists to bridge the gap between clinical practice and biomedical research. * Medical Engineering and Medical Physics (MEMP) Program: Focuses on developing cutting-edge medical technologies and therapies. Through HST, MIT students can gain exposure to medical research, clinical settings, and collaborate with leading experts in the field. This interdisciplinary approach fosters innovation and prepares students for careers in medicine, biomedical engineering, and other related fields.
